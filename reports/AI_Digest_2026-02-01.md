## 1. Executive Summary (The Pulse)
Today's technical discussions are heavily focused on the practical challenges and security risks associated with agentic AI, particularly in coding contexts. There is strong demand for better sandboxing, context management, secure tool integration, and hallucination prevention, especially in high-stakes domains like law.

The most critical concern revolves around the insufficient security measures for AI coding agents. The ability of agents to write and execute code introduces significant vulnerabilities, as current sandboxing solutions are considered inadequate. A more robust, fine-grained sandboxing environment designed specifically for AI agents is needed. This environment should incorporate network isolation, resource limits, runtime behavior monitoring, and potentially formal verification of agent-generated code to ensure safety before execution, thereby moving beyond OS-level protections and reducing the risk of unintended or malicious actions.
