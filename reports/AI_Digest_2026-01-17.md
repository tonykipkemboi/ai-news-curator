The performance penalty associated with enforcing structured outputs from LLMs (like JSON) represents a critical architectural bottleneck. LLMs are often 2-3x slower when constrained, limiting their applicability in real-time agentic systems. This stems from the iterative decoding and constraint checking needed to guarantee output conformity. A potential solution lies in exploring alternative output formats (YAML, TOML) or hardware acceleration to minimize latency, allowing faster and more efficient LLM-powered applications.
